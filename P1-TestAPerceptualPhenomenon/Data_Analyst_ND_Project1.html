P1-Test a Perceptual Phenomenon¶

Author: Chenjun Ling

Background: Stroop task¶

In a Stroop task, participants are presented with a list of words, with each word displayed in a color of ink. The participants task is to say out loud the color of the ink in which the word is printed. The task has two conditions: a congruent words condition, and an incongruent words condition. In the congruent words condition, the words being displayed are color words whose names match the colors in which they are printed: for example RED, BLUE. In the incongruent words condition, the words displayed are color words whose names do not match the colors in which they are printed: for example PURPLE, ORANGE. In each case, we measure the time it takes to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists. Each participant will go through and record a time from each condition. Details

Statistical Analysis Results¶

1. What is our independent variable? What is our dependent variable?¶

Answer: Independent--word conditions (Congruent or Incongruent); Dependent--participant respond time

2. What is an appropriate set of hypotheses for this task? What kind of statistical test do you expect to perform? Justify your choices.¶

Answer: In this experiment, we can suppose the response time for incongruent words is longer than congruent words due to the wrongly assigned color. So, an appropriate set of hypothesis is as below:

\(\mu_c\): congruent words mean respond time; \(\mu_i\): incongruent words mean respond time

The null hypothesis \(H_0:\mu_c<=\mu_i\): there is no significant difference between congruent and incongruent word respond time

The alternative hypothesis \(H_A:\mu_c>\mu_i\): respond time of incongruent tooks longer than that of congruent word

Based on the alternative hypothesis, I will conduct upper-tailed t test

5. Now, perform the statistical test and report your results. What is your confidence level and your critical statistic value? Do you reject the null hypothesis or fail to reject it? Come to a conclusion in terms of the experiment task. Did the results match up with your expectations?¶

Confidence Level: 95%, so \(\alpha\)=0.05

Degree of Freedom (DF): 24-1=23

T-critical value: based on the t table, is 1.714

T-statistic value: (\(\mu_i\)-\(\mu_c\))/SEM = (\(\mu_d\)-0)/(\(s_d\)/ \(\sqrt(n)\)) = 7.97/(4.8648/\(\sqrt(24)\)) = 8.02

Reject the null hypothesis: since I conduct upper-tailed t-test, the reject region is the upper regeion. Because t-statistic value > t-critical value, and p < 0.05, I will reject the null hypothesis, and it match up with my expectation.
